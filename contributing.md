# Contributing to YASA

There are four ways to improve YASA's data. All are free. None require a technical background.

---

## 1. Verify institution locations: YASA Verify

The most impactful contribution. Some institutions in our database have approximate coordinates (assigned to a city centre rather than the actual campus). Three independent matching submissions from different contributors trigger an automatic coordinate update.

**How:** Visit [yasa.ng/verify](https://yasa.ng/verify), register with your name, then drag the map pin to the correct campus location.

**What you earn:**
- 1 point per submission
- 5–10 bonus points when your submission contributes to a verified result
- Badges for milestones (First Verifier, Ground Truth Hero, State Champion)
- A certificate of contribution at 10 verified institutions, downloadable, shareable

**Time required:** 30 seconds per institution.

---

## 2. Correct institution data

Found a wrong website, outdated fee information, a missing course, or an incorrect accreditation status? Submit a correction directly from the institution's page on YASA.

**How:** Open any institution on [yasa.ng/app](https://yasa.ng/app), scroll to the bottom of the institution card, and tap "Report a data error on this page". Select the field you want to correct, enter the correct value, and optionally link to a source (the institution's official website or NUC page).

**Fields you can correct:**
- Official website URL
- Session fees (minimum and maximum)
- Courses offered (report a missing or incorrectly listed course)
- NUC/NBTE accreditation status
- Year established
- Student portal URL
- Campus address
- Contact phone and email
- Post-UTME type (CBT / Interview / Essay / None)
- Anything else, use the "Other" option

**Review:** All corrections are reviewed by the YASA team before affecting the live platform. We aim to review within 5 business days. You earn 1 point per correction submitted.

---

## 3. Report a missing institution

Know of a JAMB-eligible institution that does not appear on YASA? Report it.

**How:** Visit [yasa.ng/verify](https://yasa.ng/verify) and scroll to "Report a missing institution". Provide the institution's name, type, state, and any other details you have. If you have the institution's website or a link to their NUC/NBTE listing, include it.

**Who should report:**
- Students who attended or applied to an institution not appearing on YASA
- Staff or representatives of institutions that are not yet listed
- Researchers or field workers who have visited campuses not in our database

**What happens next:** Our team reviews all suggestions. Approved institutions are added to the verification queue, then to the live platform once coordinates are confirmed. You earn 2 points for each suggestion submitted.

## 4. Read yasa blog

YASA periodically updates her blog website. check [blog.yasa.ng](https://blog.yasa.ng). get updates, insights and life enriching contents centered around the Nigerian Educational system.

## Research collaboration

If you are a researcher, university department, or civic organisation interested in collaborating on YASA's dataset — co-authoring research, conducting field verification, or adapting the methodology for another country, please contact us at research@yasa.ng.

---

## What we do not accept

- Submissions that have not been personally verified (do not submit a location you haven't confirmed)
- Corrections without a traceable source for fee or accreditation data
- Suggestions for institutions you cannot independently describe (name, state, type minimum)

Low-quality submissions reduce your trust score in the system and do not earn points.

---

## Attribution

Named contributors will be acknowledged in YASA's annual data quality report. Significant contributors, those who verify 10 or more institutions, will receive a Certificate of Contribution documenting their role in improving Nigeria's educational access data infrastructure.
